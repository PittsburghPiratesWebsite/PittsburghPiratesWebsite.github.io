The Beginning of the Pittsburgh Pirates:

The Pirates were founded in 1882 and originally known as the Allegheny Base Ball Club of Pittsburgh, part of the now defunct American Association. They eventually joined the National League in 1887 and became known as the Pittsburgh Alleghenys.

They were known for their piratical ways of aquiring players, and they adopted the name "Pirates" officially for the 1891 season as the Pittsburgh Pirates we know today.

World Champions for the First Time

in 1909, the Pirates has a 110-42-2 record, they made it to the World Series that year and won against the Detroit Tigers, starting a new era in Pittsburgh, the city of champions.
